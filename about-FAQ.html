ABOUT

Welcome! Please use the menu bar to navigate between our website pages - it located at the top of every page. To locate free food sources near you, click the "Map" link to search by location, or the "Calendar" link to search by date. Both our "Map" and "Calendar" pages have helpful tips to get started. If you are not finding what you need, use the menu bar to navigate to the "Resources" page, where we detail further community resources for free-food, transportation, and support services. Enjoy!

MISSION

Free Food PDX is an organization that strives to connect the people of Portland to food pantries, free meals and urban foraging opportunities. We believe that there is more than enough food to feed our city, and that no one should go hungry simply because they cannot afford to buy groceries. This website is our response to one part of the problems within our food system: an online, easy, user-friendly resource that connects people to free food.

FAQ

How do I know if I am eligible to get free food?

  • Whether it is long term or short term, food assistance is available to anyone who needs it.
  • Some organizations require that you meet specific guidelines (ie income, proof of address, etc.) for their food services.
  • Please call organizations for further information.

What type of services can I access?

  • You may access a variety of food assistance programs such as free hot meals, food pantries, food markets, and school pantries! Some organizations may require you to meet certain guidelines.
  • Please refer to our resources page or the organization’s website for further information.

What do I need to bring with me?

  • It is recommended that you bring with you a grocery bag or boxes to carry food to your home from food markets.
  • Some food pantries require proof of address due to serving specific geographical zones of Portland.
  • For more information of specific guidelines, please refer to our resources section or the organization’s website for clarification.

What can I expect when I go to a food assistance site?

  • Expect a welcoming atmosphere! We believe that no one should be hungry therefore food assistance is available to anyone who needs it.
  • Food pantries may require a brief intake process.
  • Usually, you will find a variety of local fresh produce because everyone is deserving of healthy and fresh food.

What if I have a negative experience at a site?

  • We value your experience at the center of our actions and decisions. We resolve to listen to and include you in our work and respond to where bias and inequities appear.
  • Please contact us if you have experienced discrimination: (503) - 444 - 4242.

What if I’m unable to leave my home to get food?

  • A variety of agencies offer delivery for clients who are unable to travel to a food assistance site.
  • Food assistance sites will provide an authorized representative form to allow a family member or friend to pick up food for you and or your family.
